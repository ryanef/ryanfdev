In 2014 before I even understood anything about cloud, I was looking for a cheap way to host a Wordpress blog when I found an article talking about using AWS free tier services to get dirt cheap hosting. I followed a tutorial on spinning up an EC2 server and it worked, but like many other big ideas I had at the time, the site was quickly forgotten about and that was the end of my experience with AWS until 2020. I was long overdue on a much needed career change at the time and after talking to a colleague who works in cloud, I began studying for the AWS Solutions Architect Associate certification around September 2020.

In the beginning, I studied every day and was definitely making progress but at some point I started slacking off and missing days with studying. Just like missing days in the gym, the days turn into weeks and when you finally get back at it, you have to take a few steps back from where you left off. A few months go by and while I learned a lot of new things, I definitely wasted a lot of time as well. 

That's when I decided the one thing that could keep me on track was committing to studying by putting money on the line and registering for the exam - and it worked. It gave me a deadline and goal date to help keep me on track. So if you feel like you're studying and losing motivation along the way, maybe that's something that could help.
